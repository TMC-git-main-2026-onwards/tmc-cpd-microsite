A few notes on the design decisions:
50 minutes maps to 1 CPD hour. The CSP counts self-directed online learning generously — 50 minutes of active content (with quizzes and interactives) comfortably qualifies as 1 CPD hour. That's a clean, marketable number and a low barrier to entry. If you later want to expand to 2-3 hours (adding advanced modules on specific conditions, or a supervised practical element), you can build a tiered programme.
Each module follows the same rhythm: short nurse video to set context (2-3 min), interactive or visual content to apply the learning (3 min), then a quiz or scenario exercise to test it (2-3 min). That consistency means learners know what to expect and your production team can template the build.
The final assessment sits in Module 5 rather than being a separate step — it pulls questions from all five modules, so completion of Module 5 is effectively the programme exit exam. An 80% pass mark with unlimited retakes keeps it accessible but credible.
On the badge: Credly (now part of Pearson) is the standard for digital credentials in UK healthcare CPD. A Credly badge lets physios add it to LinkedIn with one click, which is free marketing for TMC every time someone shares it. The setup cost is modest and the ongoing per-badge cost is negligible at scale.
The referral CTA is woven in, not bolted on. Module 5 ends with a clinic finder and conversation scripts — so the natural next action after completing the programme is to actually use what they've learned. You could also offer completers a co-branded referral card (PDF or physical) they can hand to patients.
